That explains it completely. 17 of the 18 parents have zero trials and zero contracts — and the one exception (Srivathsan) has 3 trials but still no contract. None of them ever completed a paid enrollment.

So here's the answer to your question:

The fields are mandatory — but only at the final submit step of the enrollment form. The person/parent record gets created earlier, the moment someone signs in.

The flow is:

A user signs in (Google/account creation) → a person row is created immediately with just name + email (that's all OAuth gives us).
For a parent, a parent row is also created early so the app can track them.
The phone (for parents) and city/country (for students) only become required when they reach and submit the enrollment form.
If they abandon before finishing — which all 18 of these did — the record already exists with name+email but no phone, and nothing forces them back.
So these aren't corrupted or improperly-saved records. They're incomplete / abandoned signups — people who created an account but never enrolled. That's also why several look like throwaway/test accounts (Hype Byte, Jenny Jenny, Nina Cheuck @repl.it, Harper Waters). The integrity check is being overly strict: it flags every person row, even ones that never reached the step where the field is required.

The same is true for the 3 students — accounts created, onboarding never finished, so no city/country.
